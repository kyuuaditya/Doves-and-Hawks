This is a simulation of creatures where we have two types of species, Doves and Hawks. The concept shows how 2 different strategies work in a randomised enviornment and how their populations balance out completely based on deterministic calculations in this linear model. While we managed to get away easily in this time as we only had 2 different creatures. having more than 2 would be a lot more work on the way. In this linear model we only make 2 lines of expectations and the intersection points tornout to be the population ratios. Having 3 different strategies will lead to a triangle with centroid being the most ideal balancing points within all 3. The larger the triangle the bigger will be it's island of stability. "chance" factor won't play a very big role when numbers are big but surely will when food is scarse
